# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary user: a single self-tracking user maintaining daily fitness, body, diet, cardio, and training records on a Windows desktop tool.

Usage context:
- Daily quick entry at home on desktop
- Frequent correction and review after save
- Ongoing lookup of historical body, diet, and movement progress
- A complementary mobile viewer for read-only gym-side reference

Primary job to be done:
- Capture one day's fitness and nutrition data quickly
- Preserve raw text while structuring key fields
- Review, correct, and trust the saved structured record
- Revisit history without friction

## Product Purpose

Fitness Ledger is a local-first personal logging tool for body data, nutrition, cardio, training sessions, and movement history.

It exists to turn unstructured daily notes into structured records without losing the original input. The product should feel reliable, calm, and precise rather than motivational or gamified.

Success looks like:
- daily entry is fast
- review before save is trustworthy
- correction workflows are direct
- historical lookup is readable
- the interface disappears into the logging task

Emotional goals:

## Anti-references

This should not look like:
- a colorful fitness app dashboard
- an admin panel
- an analytics console
- a spreadsheet disguised as product UI
- stacked generic cards and boxed KPI blocks
- loud startup gradients or decorative SaaS chrome

Avoid specifically:
- visible table-first presentation as the main user experience
- heavy borders, equal rectangles, and dashboard grids
- gamified or commercial-fitness visual language
- Windows-default control styling as the visible design language

## Design Principles

1. Logging is the primary task
   The input, review, correction, and lookup flow must stay centered on practical daily use.

2. Structure should emerge from space, not boxes
   Grouping, hierarchy, and emphasis should come from spacing, rhythm, and typography before containers.

3. Familiarity beats novelty in product UI
   The tool should feel trustworthy and legible first, with refinement added through restraint rather than invention.

4. Raw input is sacred, structured output is editable
   The product must preserve the user's original entry while making the structured record easy to inspect and correct.

5. Quiet premium over decorative premium
   The interface should feel high-end through material restraint, proportion, and clarity, not through visual noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Prioritize readable contrast over low-contrast elegance
- Preserve clear focus states for inputs and controls
- Keep motion subtle and state-driven only
- Avoid relying on color alone for meaning
- Support extended reading and correction sessions without visual fatigue
